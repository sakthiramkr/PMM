Product Influence VoC Research Roadmap
From Customer Signals to Roadmap Prioritisation
Aggregating signals across four data sources surfaced a product gap, built a business case, and moved a feature from backlog to beta in one quarter — restoring multi-million-dollar deal velocity.
4
Signal Sources
1 Qtr
Backlog → Beta
20%
Enterprise Win Rate

TrainerCentral's pipeline was growing but late-stage enterprise deals kept stalling. No one had a systematic view of why. I built a signal aggregation engine pulling from four sources simultaneously, cross-referencing patterns to find the consistent blocker.

01 · SIGNAL
G2 & Capterra Reviews
Mined recurring themes in 1-star reviews and competitor comparisons
02 · SIGNAL
Reddit Communities
Monitored L&D forums for unfiltered frustration and competitor switching signals
03 · SIGNAL
Support Ticket Themes
Analysed ticket volume and escalation patterns for product gap signals
04 · SIGNAL
CRM Lost-Deal Notes
Structured analysis of why deals were marked lost across 6 months

A clear pattern emerged: SCORM compliance was the consistent blocker across all four sources. Enterprise buyers couldn't migrate content from existing authoring tools without it. Every major competitor had it. I compiled a business case — competitive analysis, cross-source signal volume, blocked pipeline estimate — and presented it to the product team. SCORM was prioritised within one quarter and shipped to beta, deployed first to accounts that had flagged it directly.

→ Why this transfers to payments

The same signal aggregation method applies directly to merchant acquiring — where product gaps (missing payment methods, slow settlement, poor reconciliation UX) are often the real deal-killers that never surface in structured win/loss calls. Method, not domain, is the skill.

The original flat pricing had no feature caps — power users sat on entry-level plans with no natural reason to upgrade. I started with structured research: customer interviews mapping real usage, CRM usage data finding where customers were outgrowing plans, and a competitor pricing teardown.

01 · RESEARCH
Willingness-to-Pay
1:1 interviews and usage data to map segment value perception
02 · DESIGN
Feature Cap Architecture
Designed upgrade pressure at key usage thresholds across tiers
03 · TEST
Cohort Testing

04 · LAUNCH
Enterprise Tier
SSO, encryption, analytics — used in late-stage deal conversations

Revenue lifted 18% across two quarters — driven by new conversions to mid-tier and upgrade pressure on existing power users who had outgrown the entry plan. Enterprise plan became a deal-closer before public launch.

→ Why this transfers to payments

Pricing strategy in merchant acquiring — MDR structures, interchange-plus, flat-rate vs. tiered plans — follows the same principles: segment willingness-to-pay, competitive anchoring, and feature-gating to drive upgrade behaviour.
